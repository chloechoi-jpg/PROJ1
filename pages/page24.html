Morning Light (Ending)

You wake with a sharp breath, sunlight touching the walls of your room. The details of the dream scatter immediately—paths, voices, silver skies slipping through your grasp. You tell yourself it was only a dream, something your mind created and discarded. Yet throughout the day, fragments return uninvited. A scent, a sound, the feeling of standing somewhere just out of reach. That night, exhaustion pulls you back into sleep. As your eyes close, the darkness does not stay dark for long. The ground beneath you becomes soft. Grass glimmers faintly. The sky opens. You are back at the edge of the dream.
